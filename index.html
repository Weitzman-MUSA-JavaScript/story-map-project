Philadelphia Ethnic Food Clusters

Moving off of the University of Pennsylvania's campus during the Covid-19 pandemic, I found myself in a drastically different food environment.

In my new neighborhood of Spruce Hill, Philadelphia, I discovered the succulent chicken sandwiches at Saad's Halal Restaurant, the tender lamb leg at Hadramout, and discovered a wealth of other middle eastern and halal eateries.

As a spatial data enthusiast, this led me to wonder how the ethnic food landscape of Philadelphia varies across geographies.

This storymap documents what I've found through a year of research and data collection from Yelp's Fusion API and the Encyclopedia of Greater Philadelphia.

Philadelphia Restaurants, according to Yelp

I identified Yelp as the most up-to-date and granular source of information on open restaurants, due to the business incentive for restaurants to keep their listings current. Yelp also relies on crowdsourcing to verify whether businesses are still open.

In total, there are 2,288 total ethnic restaurants in Philadelphia based on Yelp's Fusion API. Using their description fields and names, I keyword mapped cuisines for restaurants.

Following is the frequency of the cuisine categories in the city.

Top cuisine by census tract

Aggregated to the tract level, we see that the most common cuisine in East Asian. It is difficult, however, to see the distribution of other cuisine groups due to the sheer number of East Asian restaurants.

East Asian

East Asian cuisine is the most common ethnic cuisine category in Philadelphia. The most common sub-category is Chinese, followed by Japanese.

These cuisines generally cluster in Center City and University City, close to Chinatown and the University of Pennsylvania, although Chinese restaurants spread throughout the city.

Mediterranean

The primary types of mediterranean food in the city are Italian and Greek.

Most restaurants in this category are clustered in Center City and parts of South Philadelphia, although there are pockets of Italian food in Northeast Philadelphia.

Latin American

Latin American food in Philadelphia is dominated by Mexican restaurants, followed by unspecified Latin American and Brazilian restaurants.

There are clusters in South, West, and North Philadelphia east of Broad Street.

Southeast Asian

Southeast Asian food clusters primarily in South Philadelphia and Center City, East of Broad Street.

The primary cuisines are Thai and Vietnamese.

Caribbean

Caribbean food in Philadelphia is predominantly in West, Southwest, and North Philadelphia west of Broad street.

The primary cuisine is Jamaican, followed by restaurants that are a mix of Caribbean cuisines.

European

The main European cuisines are British and Irish. Most of these restaurants cluster in Center City.

Middle Eastern

Middle Eastern food clusters mainly in Center City and West Philadelphia.

Compared to the other cuisine groups, there is substantial overlap between sub-groups, with the majority of restaurants lacking cuisine-specific details.

South Asian

South Asian food in Philadelphia is primarily Indian, with a strong presence in West Philadelphia.

African

Most African restaurants in Philadelphia are not specific to one country.

The most prevalent single cuisine is Ethiopian, which has a strong presence in West Philadelphia.
