SERPONG-BALARAJA TOLL ROAD

Serpong-Balaraja Toll Road is one of Indonesia's National Strategic Projects with a length of 39.95 km.

Extended from Serpong, South Tangerang City to Balaraja, Tangerang District, people will be able to travel and move goods faster from Tangerang District to South Tangerang City without having to pass the busiest toll road, Jakarta-Merak.

PHASE 1

Phase 1 spans as long as 9.3 km and passes 1 administrative village in the city of South Tangerang and 6 administrative villages in the district of Tangerang. It has 1.221 lands with 98% private properties, 1% government-own land, and 1% religious bequest land.

Half of phase 1 has operated, from KM 0 to KM 5, with the road officially inaugurated by The President of Indonesia in 2022. Meanwhile, the rest of phase 1 will operate at the end of this year.

LAND ACQUISITION PROGRESS

Land Acquisition for Serpong-Balaraja Toll Road Phase 1 has been going on from 2016 to 2024, with 1.216 lands (99.59%) has been acquired.

Most of the compensation for releasing land ownership rights was in the form of money. Meanwhile, the government-owned and religious bequest lands were acquired by replacing the land in another location with the same price value.

GOVERNMENT-OWNED LAND

This is the land of the Tangerang District government, with an area of 1.538 km2.

Right now, it is in the middle of an appraisal process to find the right replacement location with a land price similar to the appraisal of the original land.

PHASE 2

Land Acquisition of Serpong-Balaraja Toll Road Phase 2 will start next year. The road spans 11.5 km across 10 administrative villages.

The total area for phase 2 is 8.4 hectares. The types of land ownership in Phase 2 consist of private properties (99%), village-owned land, and cemeteries.

PHASE 3

Land Acquisition for Serpong-Balaraja Toll Road Phase 3 will start simultaneously with Phase 2. The road spans 19.159 km across 20 administrative villages.

The type of land for this phase consists of private properties (98%), public schools, village-own land, and cemeteries.

The toll road land acquisition is expected to finish by 2025 and start operating fully by 2027.
